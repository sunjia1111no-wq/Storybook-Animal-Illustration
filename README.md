# Storybook Animal Illustration

## Overview

Use this skill to create or prompt illustrations in a whimsical, hand-painted picture-book style: anthropomorphic animals with theatrical silhouettes, dry textured brushwork, scratchy fur lines, vintage clothing, and soft muted backgrounds.

Do not copy a reference image exactly. Preserve the style language while changing the subject, pose, clothing, and composition as the user requests.

## Style Recipe

### Character Design

- Use anthropomorphic animals with clear animal anatomy and human-like posture, clothing, and expressions.
- Favor exaggerated silhouettes: long narrow legs, hunched backs, oversized hats, tiny hands, long snouts, floppy limbs, round eyes, or stretched torsos.
- Make the pose slightly awkward, theatrical, or mid-action, as if captured during a funny story moment.
- Keep facial expressions readable and comic: worried, smug, startled, grumpy, delighted, or mischievous.
- Give characters personality through clothing: plaid shirts, ruffled dresses, vests, patched trousers, wide-brim hats, scarves, collars, cuffs, boots, or tiny accessories.

### Line And Edge

- Use loose, uneven contour edges rather than polished vector outlines.
- Add scratchy, searching construction lines inside forms, especially on fur, cloth folds, hats, tails, and snouts.
- Let fine stray hairs extend beyond the silhouette, especially around cheeks, ears, tails, collars, cuffs, and fuzzy bodies.
- Use thin pale whisker-like strokes and short dark hatch marks for texture.
- Keep the drawing lively and imperfect; avoid clean graphic outlines, airbrushed edges, or overly symmetrical shapes.

### Brushwork And Texture

- Build forms with layered dry-brush strokes, visible grain, and scumbled paint texture.
- Use semi-opaque strokes that let underpainting and paper-like tooth show through.
- Add dense texture in fur and fabric, lighter texture in smooth skin, eyes, noses, and simple backgrounds.
- Use directional strokes to describe volume: vertical strokes on trousers, curved strokes on hats and snouts, radiating strokes on fans, collars, or ruffles.
- Add small highlights with rough pale strokes, not glossy digital shine except on wet noses or eyes.

### Color Palette

- Prefer muted, warm, slightly vintage colors over high-saturation digital color.
- Common background tones: warm peach, pale beige, dusty sage, parchment tan, light cream.
- Useful character colors: fox orange, russet brown, moss green, mustard yellow, faded denim blue, dusty periwinkle, brick red, plum purple, warm gray, bone white.
- Use one or two stronger clothing colors as anchors, then balance them with earthy neutrals.
- Keep shadows colored and soft: blue-gray, brown-violet, muted olive, or warm umber. Avoid pure black except for small eye or nostril accents.

### Composition

- Place characters on a plain matte background with no heavy scenery unless the user asks for environment.
- Leave generous negative space around the figure.
- Use asymmetrical cropping and scale shifts when funny: giant hat, tiny body, long tail, huge snout, or character partly entering the frame.
- For multiple characters, stage them diagonally or in a comic physical relationship: tumbling, balancing, chasing, leaning, hiding, or reacting.
- Keep the image readable at thumbnail size: clear silhouette first, texture second.

## Prompt Pattern

When writing an image prompt, include these parts in order:

1. Subject and story moment.
2. Anthropomorphic character shape and expression.
3. Clothing and props.
4. Style terms: hand-painted picture book illustration, loose dry-brush texture, scratchy expressive linework, visible paper tooth, soft matte background.
5. Palette and background color.
6. Composition notes and negative constraints.

Example:

```text
An anthropomorphic hedgehog tailor standing on tiptoe to measure an enormous velvet sleeve, worried but determined expression, tiny round spectacles, patched moss-green waistcoat, rolled trousers, pins tucked into the cuff. Hand-painted picture book illustration with loose dry-brush texture, scratchy expressive linework, visible paper tooth, fine stray quills and fur hairs, soft matte parchment background. Muted vintage palette of moss green, russet brown, faded denim blue, cream, and warm umber. Generous negative space, slightly awkward theatrical pose, no clean vector outlines, no photorealism, no glossy 3D rendering.
```

## Negative Direction

Avoid:

- Photorealism, 3D render, anime, manga, flat vector, sticker art, overly polished children's cartoon style.
- Smooth gradients, plastic shading, hard black outlines, neon colors, symmetrical mascot design.
- Busy scenic backgrounds that compete with the character.
- Exact reuse of the reference images' characters, poses, outfits, or compositions unless the user explicitly asks to recreate their own provided image.

## Quick Checklist

Before finalizing a prompt or illustration direction, confirm:

- The animal is expressive, anthropomorphic, and story-driven.
- The silhouette is quirky and readable.
- The clothing feels vintage, textured, and character-specific.
- Brushwork is dry, layered, and visibly imperfect.
- Lines are scratchy and alive, with stray hairs or hatch marks.
- Colors are muted, warm, and balanced against a plain soft background.
